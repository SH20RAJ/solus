### Template 2: Cold Email to Alt-VCs (Purpose/Zebra Fund)
**Subject:** Pitch: Solus — The Personal Social Network (Anti-Attention Economy)

```text
Hi [Investor Name],

I hope this email finds you well.

I’ve been following your investments at [Fund Name], specifically your focus on high-trust platforms and ethical tech models.

I am the founder of Solus (https://github.com/SH20RAJ/solus). We are building the world’s first Personal Social Network.

The Problem:
Modern social media is performative. Users are exhausted by broadcasting for likes, comments, and algorithms. Millions perform "digital detoxes," but when they leave, they lose the habit of documenting their lives entirely.

The Solution:
Solus replicates the familiar, beautiful UX of modern social feeds (photos, videos, stories, location maps) but everything is strictly private by default. There is no audience, no followers, and no algorithm. Users build a digital autobiography for themselves, with the option to securely publish specific collections (e.g., "90 Days building my startup") only when they are ready.

Traction & Roadmap:
- Core product is fully built and deployed (Next.js 16 Edge, Cloudflare R2, TypeScript, Better Auth).
- Upcoming features include local AI face-recognition, auto-album generation, and an introspective AI "subconscious self" that comments privately to prompt writing depth.

We are raising a pre-seed round of [Amount] to accelerate our product roadmap and launch our organic waitlist.

I would love to send over our brief pitch deck and schedule a 10-minute call next week to discuss this further.

Thank you for your time and dedication to building a healthier internet.

Warm regards,

[Your Name]
Founder, Solus
GitHub: https://github.com/SH20RAJ/solus
```

### Template 3: Journalist Pitch (Substack/Newsletters)
**Subject:** Story Idea: The social network built for an audience of one

```text
Hi [Journalist Name],

I read your recent article on [recent topic/digital fatigue], and it resonated deeply.

I wanted to share a project I've launched that tackles this problem from a new angle: Solus (https://github.com/SH20RAJ/solus).

Solus is a "personal social network." It gives users the familiar, rich media-upload experience of Instagram and Snapchat, but removes the audience entirely. There are no followers, no public likes, and no comments. Everything is private by default. 

Our core philosophy is: "Live first. Share later." You document your life privately, and choose to publish specific themed collections (like travel logs, fitness logs, or coding updates) only when you are ready.

I think this represents a shift in consumer behavior that your readers would find interesting—moving away from performative broadcasting back to raw, private self-documentation.

I’d be happy to give you a private tour of the application or share our founding story.

Best regards,

[Your Name]
Founder, Solus
```

### Template 4: NLnet / Mozilla Grant Short Proposal
**Project Name:** Solus
**One-Sentence Abstract:** A secure, private-first personal media platform enabling data-sovereign digital self-documentation without algorithmic surveillance.

**Detailed Description:**
```text
Solus addresses the societal problem of performative digital spaces. Current social platforms monetize user attention by maximizing validation loops (likes, followers, public comments), which degrades mental health and forces users to trade their privacy for self-expression.

Solus provides a secure alternative by decoupling documentation from public broadcasting. Built on a decentralized-ready, edge-first Next.js architecture, Solus stores media (via R2/S3 storage buckets) and timeline data privately. All features (chronological feeds, ephemeral stories, geo-tagging, tag cataloging) are private-by-default. Users retain complete control over their database and can optionally generate static, encrypted shareable links to select folders, bypassing standard surveillance-capitalism social engines.
```
