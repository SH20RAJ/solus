# Solus — Design Vision & UX Principles

> **Leave everyone. Don't leave yourself.**

This document defines *why* Solus looks and feels the way it does. Every other
document in `docs/` inherits from the principles below. When a decision is
ambiguous, return here.

---

## 1. Vision

Solus is a **private-first social platform** where people document their lives
without an audience. It recreates the familiar experience of social media —
posts, stories, photos, videos, and a personal timeline — but removes the
pressure of likes, followers, comments, and algorithms.

The default experience is solitude. Users can *later* choose to publish part or
all of their journey.

> Instagram asks: *"What will people think?"*
> Solus asks: *"What do you think?"*

---

## 2. Design Philosophy

**Calm. Intentional. Minimal. Human.**

Solus should never feel like Instagram, Facebook, or Twitter. It should feel
like **opening a personal journal**.

The emotional target for every screen:

> "I can breathe."

We never excite. We never overwhelm. We never manipulate. Every screen should
make the user *slower*, not faster.

---

## 3. Core UX Principles

### 3.1 Content First
The UI disappears. The user's memories are the hero. Chrome, controls, and
navigation recede until needed.

### 3.2 White Space Wins
Always prefer **more spacing** over **more components**. Emptiness is a feature,
not a gap to fill.

### 3.3 One Primary Action
Every screen has exactly **one** clear primary action — never five. Secondary
actions are quiet.

### 3.4 No Attention Economy
- No red badges
- No unread counters
- No streak pressure
- No "You missed…" nudges
- No dopamine hacks

### 3.5 Familiar Yet Different
Users should instantly understand *how* to use the app (familiar patterns), but
it should *feel* emotionally different (calm, private, unhurried).

---

## 4. The Three Questions

Every interaction must be able to answer yes to:

1. **Can this be simpler?**
2. **Can this require fewer taps?**
3. **Can this feel calmer?**

---

## 5. Target Users

- Solo travelers
- Students preparing for competitive exams
- Startup founders
- Creators taking a break
- People on digital detoxes
- People recovering from burnout or breakups
- Anyone who wants to rediscover themselves

---

## 6. Core User Flow

```
Open App
   ↓
Timeline
   ↓
Create Memory
   ↓
Save
   ↓
Continue Living
```

No interruptions. No detours. No dead ends designed to keep users in-app.

---

## 7. Non-Goals

Solus deliberately does **not** pursue:

- Competing with Instagram
- Endless scrolling
- Trending content
- An influencer economy
- Engagement / screen-time optimization
- Public discovery or a global feed

---

## 8. Design Inspiration

Not Instagram. Not Facebook. Closer to:

- Apple Journal
- Apple Photos "Memories"
- Notion
- Arc Browser
- Linear
- Raycast
- Day One
- Minimal iOS system apps

---

## 9. Design North Star

> If a screen feels busy, remove something.
> If it still feels busy, remove another thing.

The best interface for Solus is the one that quietly gets out of the user's way
and lets their memories become the focus.

---

## 10. Success Definition

A successful user is **not** the one who spends the most time in Solus. It is the
one who builds a timeline they genuinely value years later.
